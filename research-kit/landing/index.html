How it works

From search to verified draft — in minutes

1

Search on your platform

Navigate to Elicit, SciSpace or Consensus and run your research query as usual.

2

Claims auto-detected

Research Kit detects all claims on the page and verifies each one against the source paper via AI.

3

Save to Inbox

One click saves verified claims to your project inbox, grouped by paper and ready to review.

4

Generate your draft

Select claims from your inbox and let AI compose a citation-backed draft in seconds.

Features

Everything you need for rigorous research

Built for academics, students, and knowledge workers who need to trust their sources.

Auto-verify claims

Detects claims on Elicit, SciSpace and Consensus and checks them against source papers using AI — no manual copy-paste needed.

Organized Inbox

Verified claims land in your project inbox, grouped by paper with status badges. Select and archive in bulk.

AI Draft writing

Select claims from your inbox and generate a citation-backed draft in Short, Default or Long format — ready to copy and edit.

Conflict detection

Automatically flags contradictory findings and surfaces structured error messages with AI-suggested resolutions — powered by fallback LLM routing for 99.9% uptime.

Chat with papers

Ask follow-up questions about any paper in your project and get grounded answers with source references.

Upload inaccessible PDFs

Paper behind a paywall? Upload the PDF manually and Research Kit verifies claims against your local copy.

Setup

Installation guide

Takes about 2 minutes. No developer experience needed.

1

Download the extension

Click the Download Extension button above and save the .zip file somewhere you can find it.

2

Unzip the file

Extract the .zip to a permanent folder (e.g. ~/extensions/research-kit). Don't delete the folder after installation — Chrome needs it.

3

Open Chrome Extensions

Navigate to chrome://extensions and enable Developer mode with the toggle in the top-right corner.

4

Load the extension

Click Load unpacked and select the folder from step 2. The Research Kit icon appears in your Chrome toolbar.

5

Sign in and create a project

Click the Research Kit icon to open the sidebar, sign in with your Google account, and create your first project. Navigate to Elicit, SciSpace or Consensus — claims are detected and verified automatically.
